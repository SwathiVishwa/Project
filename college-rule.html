General Rules

  • College works from Monday to Friday and on first, third and fifth Saturdays.
  • Working Hours: 09.00 a.m. – 05.00 p.m.
  • As per the Time Table prescribed from time to time
    • Interval (FN) : 10.40 a.m. – 10.55 a.m. / 11.30 a.m. – 11.45 a.m.
    • Lunch Break : 12.35 p.m – 01.25 p.m. / 01.25 p.m – 02.15 p.m.
    • Interval (AN) : 03.05 p.m – 03.20 p.m. / 03.55 p.m. – 04.10 p.m.
  • The periods in the Morning and Afternoon sessions are of 50 minutes duration.
  • Students are not permitted to enter into the College campus after 8.55 a.m. and are not allowed to leave the College campus before 5.00 p.m.
  • Students should follow strict timings for the class hours as per the given timetable.
  • Parents, Guardians and Visitors are strictly not allowed to meet the students during the College working hours.
  • Students should obey the Rules and Regulations prescribed by the Anna University.
  • All requisition letters for Bonafide certificates, Scholarship forms and Charitable Trust Scholarship forms should be addressed to the Principal through HOD.
  • Each and every student will have a student Counsellor. Students are requested to approach their Counsellor for their academic needs.
  • Parents are requested to feel free and spend their time for discussions with the student Counsellor / Class Advisor / HOD / Principal.
  • If there is any change in the address, contact number and Email ID, Parents should inform their respective Class Counsellor.
  • Usages of Mobile Phones, Video gadgets, IPods etc., by the students are strictly prohibited inside the College campus.
  • The Hostel students should obey the hostel rules and regulations.
  • Buses ply in various routes according to the convenience of the students, so that,they can board the bus at their nearest boarding point.
  • The vehicle of the students should be parked in the allotted parking place only.
  • Under the Central Motor Vehicle Act, wearing helmet has been made compulsory. This rule is implemented strictly to all the two – wheeler riders and pillion riders.

Conduct & Discipline:

  • The conduct of each student should be satisfactory.
  • They should be punctual to classes and do their work with devotion. No student can enter or leave the classroom as he/she likes.
  • Students should maintain discipline in the College campus. In case of any in-disciplinary action or misbehavior in any manner, the Disciplinary Committee appointed by the Principal will enquire on the matter and recommend necessary action.
  • Students should use the facilities available in the College campus without causing any damage. If any damage is done to furniture, equipment, electrical fittings, library books, College buildings etc. action will be taken to impose punishment and recover the cost.
  • Students are expected to behave in a cultured way to maintain the high status of this prestigious Institution.

    • Attendance & Leave:

  • All requisition letters for Leave should be addressed to the HOD through Class Advisor.
  • All requisition letters for Medical certificates, Special Permissions, Project Permissions and Industrial Visit Permissions should be addressed to the Principal through HOD.
  • Attendance shall be calculated on hourly basis i.e., in terms of each lecture period.
  • Attendance is compulsory for the Regular Classes, Tests and Examinations .
  • Unauthorised absence is not permitted. However, leave letters for one or two days, with Parent’s signature will be considered. If physical ailment exceeds more than two days, a medical certificate has to be produced along with the leave application.
  • Notification given by The Controller of Examinations, Anna University for Lack of Attendance / Medical Leave.
  • 75% and above overall attendance is eligible for Examination.
  • 65% & above and below 75% are considered in exceptional cases where the students are away on Medical grounds.
  • Such absenteeism on genuine medical grounds need to be forwarded to the University as and when occurs and not at the end of the semester.
  • The Principal will scrutinize the application submitted by the students and reject cases which are not genuine (like 3 days of headache &fever, Medical Certificate for Saturday, Sunday etc.)
  • Further students who have not registered for the Examination due to lack of attendance are not eligible to proceed to the next Semester.

    • Dress Code:

  • Students should wear neat and proper dress inside the College campus.
  • Girls can wear either saree or churidhar with dhupatta neatly pinned.
  • Boys should wear formal pants and shirts neatly tucked in and they should wear shoes compulsorily. Jeans, Cargos &T–shirts are not allowed inside the College campus during the working hours.
  • The students should wear the prescribed uniform for laboratory classes.
  • Every student is provided with an Identity Card. All the students should wear their Identity Card along with rope inside the College campus.

Library Rules

  • Absolute silence must be observed in the Library
  • Staff and Students members of SGIT are only allowed to use the library resources outsiders are not allowed without permission from Principal.
  • Staff and Students should wear their ID- Card with rope in the E-Gate Register Scanner while entering and leaving the Library.
  • In case of lose of ID card, the student get a permission letter from the HOD.
  • Students should come with formal dress code as per the college rules and regulations.
  • Library Books will be issued to the Staff Students only by Producing their own ID Card. Since ID cards are not transferable &Books will not
  • issued by producing other members ID Card.
  • Staffs members can avail 6 books at a time for a period of Six months and return the Library Books within the Due Date.
  • A student will be issued maximum of 4 books for U.G Degree and 6 Books for P.G. Degree. The students should return the books within 14 working da
  • ys from the date of issue.
  • While taking the books Staff and Students should write their Reg.No/Roll No. which is mentioned in their ID- Cards, on the due date card inserted in the book.
  • Students can renew the books only once and it should be done on or before the due date.
  • A fine amount of rupees 2 /- per day will be charged for a book if it is returned after the due date.
  • The returned books shall not be reissued to the same person on the same day of return.
  • Books are required to be presented physically at the Counter for return.
  • Borrowers are expected to return the books on or before the due date stamped on the Due Date slip.
  • Students are not allowed to carry books, bags, and other personal belongings inside the library. They should leave their belongings at the shelves provided and white sheets are allowed to take notes.
  • If any book issued to the student is found damaged; he/she will be responsible to replace the book.
  • If any book is lost by the borrower is not able to replace the book lost, within two weeks otherwise thrice the amount of the original cost will be charged. If a book is replaced, current edition of the book only be accepted.
  • Reference books and periodicals, Project Reports will not be issued under any circumstances.
  • Sub-lending or transfer of book is prohibited.
  • If any book is taken out from the shelf, it should be replaced in the original place.
  • Students should not alter the arrangement of books in the shelves. Any kind of marking, underlining etc., in the Books and Periodicals is absolutely forbidden.
  • Staff &Students can access E-Journals, National Programme on Technology Enhanced Learning (NPTEL), and Developing Library Network (DELNET) from our library. But other kind of online searching is Strictly Prohibited.
  • Students/Staffs who leave the college for any reason in the middle of the term should return the Library book and get No Due Certificate before leaving the college.
  • Staff and Students requested to avoid taking or discussion that will disturb other readers.
  • Library Chairs and tables should not alter from their position.
  • Readers are requested to switch off their mobile phones.

Hostel Rules

  • Admission to hostel is not automatic. Students desirous of seeking admission to the hostel should apply well before the commencement of the academic year. Admission is strictly restricted only to those who agree to abide by the rules and regulations of the hostel from time to time.
  • The student is expected not to indulge in any undesirable activity thereby causing disturbance to the smooth functioning of the hostel.
  • During the working days, students should attend the College without fail. No student should stay in the hostel during the working hours of the College. They should leave the hostel in time to attend to classes for the day.
  • Attendance will be taken by the Deputy Warden every evening.
  • The study hours in the hostel is from 8.30 p.m. to 10.30 p.m. on all days and in addition 9.00 a.m. to 12.00 noon on holidays. During the study hours, the students are expected to stay in their rooms and keep their doors open. They should avoid chatting or roaming during the study hours.
  • The Parents / Guardians, who wish to visit their ward should produce the relevant visitor’s card before entering the Hostel.
  • The inmates are allowed to visit their home once in a month.
  • Students will be permitted to go to their native place only after obtaining permission from the warden by filling in the prescribed form which is available with the Warden.
  • In Girls hostel, the students are allowed to go home only with their parents or guardian.
  • Students going to their hometown or outing on a holiday should enter the date and time of leaving the hostel in the prescribed register as well as their returning date and time.
  • All the students going for outing should return to the hostel before 6.30 p.m.
  • The hostel fee collected at the beginning of the year is for the complete academic year. In case of any student leaving the hostel on his own or expelled from the hostel in the middle, the fee will not be refunded on any account.
  • The furniture, equipment, electrical gadgets and electrical fittings provided to the students should be properly maintained and if any damage is done, action will be taken to recover the cost.
  • Smoking or use of liquor or any other intoxicant or playing cards is strictly prohibited. The guilty will be expelled from the hostel with immediate effect.
  • Management has all rights to expel the student if they are indulging in any in-disciplinary activities / misbehavior in any manner or not abiding by the hostel rules & regulations.
